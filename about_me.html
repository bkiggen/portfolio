About Me:

Interests:

I am passionate about learning. I want to know everything about everything. That the human mind can create a working model of the world, that shapes on paper can translate other people's models into mine, and that I can have any sense that those things are going on is what I live for. Computers are fascinating to me because they're humanity's attempt at recreating that inner world in something inorganic.

Skills:

I'm something of a dabbler. Something of a dirty word, I know, but I wear the moniker with some pride. While I'm not having deep conversations in Spanish, Korean, German, Turkish, and Japanese, I can order a beer and hail a cab in any of those languages. While I'm no Yo Yo Ma, I can make the guitar, ukulele, piano, drums, and bass sound pretty nice. And while I don't yet have one coding language mastered, I am well on my way to developing skills in git, github, html, css, javascript, and react.

Background:

I began my professional life in the world of Psychology. The jobs I held in my early 20's after earning my BA had me managing the daily lives and wellbeings of 33 people with severe, chronic mental illness. It was intense and fascinating and rewarding.

After leaving psychology, I came upon an opportunity to live and work in South Korea. I was a preschool teacher at an international school in Seoul. It was a great experience and started me on my path to education.

After Korea, I got my master's degree in education at Concordia University. I then taught 2nd and 3rd grades for Portland Public Schools. It was a job I felt really good about and it was hard to leave but I knew I needed a new challenge.

So I saved my pennies, studied computers and code, and eventually enrolled in Epicodus' React Track. I'm really excited to see what I can learn to create!
